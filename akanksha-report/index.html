The Akanksha School Project Turns 10!


In 2007, Akanksha aimed to create a model for high performing schools through The School Project, in collaboration with the Pune Municipal Corporation. Over the past 10 years, we have grown to 20 schools across Mumbai and Pune serving over 7400 children, in partnership with the PMC, PCMC, and the MCGM.

Teacher Engagement Survey

We conduct School Culture Survey to draw insights on three key domains - relationship with leadership, mutual trust and respect, and teacher well being. The survey helps support the School Leaders to continuously strive towards maintaining a healthy school culture in alignment with our organisation’s values.

A letter from Saurabh Taneja

CEO, The Akanksha Foundation


Akanksha has always been on a continuous path of learning about the purpose of education in a rapidly changing world. The year 2017-2018 was a year of ‘Looking Ahead’ for all of us. Building on Akanksha’s 27 years of experience during the previous two phases, 1991-2007 when Akanksha ran the after-school centres and 2007-2017 when we transitioned into running 20 schools, we wanted to define what the next decade in Akanksha’s journey would look like. We asked ourselves two fundamental questions:

1. What would define excellent education in the coming ten years and hence, what would an Akanksha graduate look like?

2. How do we further our vision of serving all children?

While exploring answers to the above questions, we reached out to all our stakeholders - from the children in our classrooms to the board members of Akanksha. After a year long process, we emerged with a revised mission of Akanksha- to build one of the largest network of innovative schools that empowers children to maximise their potential and that influences systemic reform. As we continue to strengthen our schools and deepen our impact, we have taken a critical look at how we can develop 21st century knowledge, skills and values in our children. This helped us to craft a Student Vision framework that envisions all Akanksha students to graduate with a sense of Mastery, Autonomy and Purpose, empowered with essential knowledge and skills like critical thinking, creativity, collaboration and communication with a deep focus on our core values.
Today, while we are proud of how far our network of schools have reached , we also acknowledge how far we have to go. We believe the learning taking place in each classroom can be more rigorous and fun for each child and needs to be better differentiated to address the diverse needs of all the students in our classrooms.
The School Development Review (SDR) plays a monumental role in achieving this by providing consistent support to our school teams and equipping them with the skills and resources they need, to work with each child and improve their practises. This year we incorporated the Akanksha pillars to the School Development Review for deeper understanding of each school goals. This has surfaced Akanksha’s best practises and brought attention to very specific focus areas, which we would not have realised otherwise.
As we embark on this new phase in Akanksha’s journey our aim will be to build a stronger organisation culture during this time of change that is inclusive, transparent and empowering. We believe that our values will form the foundation for us to work towards the ambitious goals we have in front of us, enjoy the process, learn from our mistakes, take pride in our achievements and innovate for the future.

Nutrition and Health Care

Starting in 2016, Akanksha’s SMCs launched an intensive nutrition and health care initiative across nine schools. They registered 750 Akanksha families to receive free check-ups and any prescribed medications. The SMCs also implemented an in-school nutrition program to prevent malnutrition. School social workers use tools such as growth charts and dietary plans, monitor tiffin contents for lunch and snack breaks and host workshops to educate parents about nutrition. Currently, all Akanksha schools in Mumbai have an ongoing nutrition program

Building Bridges

Many parents struggle to connect with their children amid busy lives due to their long work hours. Parents at Akanksha’s D.N. Nagar School raised this issue in their SMC. They developed a simple but powerful exercise, called Building Bridges, where a parent and child sit across from each other and answer, “What do I love and respect most about you?” And, “What is the one thing I will do for you this year?” These commitments are put up on a painted tree at the entrance to the school. Each day when students and parents arrive they are greeted by their commitments to one another as a reminder of this important bond.

Empowering Parents

Our parents and SMCs are also impacting government-run schools. A member of Akanksha’s Bopodi SMC also had a child at a local government-run school. She was shocked to find that despite having many issues, including a dilapidated structure and malnourished students, the school’s SMC was not active. With the help of other Akanksha parents, she went door-to-door and spoke to parents in the community about how they could make a difference at their children’s school. As a result, the government school now has an active SMC that has created a nutrition program and is working on other issues.

Support Groups for Women

In 2015-16 Akanksha formed its first Women’s Support Groups, focused on building self-confidence, leadership and workforce skills and economic independence. As part of the program, partnering colleges and organizations—such as Majlis Legal Center, Animedh Charitable Trust and Sophia College—offer educational workshops and workforce training in a variety of fields. In one group, responding to popular demand, women participants learned how to drive. Currently, six Akanksha schools operate support groups, serving 150 women.

Ending Abuse

Several SMCs identified physical abuse at home as a major issue facing their communities. Akanksha’s Laxmi Nagar School conducted a survey that revealed 87% of students were physically abused at home. The parents in the SMC worked to educate other parents about the impact of physical abuse on children. They surveyed parents on what motivates abuse, and 90% revealed they did not know how to deal with the pressures they face in their own lives.


The school created a plan to identify and support parents in need of help, working with them to develop positive disciplining systems. This year, the rate of abuse was reduced by 40%, and the school’s effort continues.


At Akanksha’s Sitaram Mill School, the SMC worked with a local NGO to run a campaign on child sexual abuse awareness and prevention. The campaign engaged children and parents in their community of 56,000 people through posters, stands, plays and dialogue. The SMC used community cricket games to engage parents, and specifically fathers, in the sexual abuse awareness campaign. More Akanksha SMCs are continuing to start their own campaigns around this issue.

Leadership Academy


The Akanksha School Leadership Academy aims to create transformational leaders who will overcome the challenges of educational inequity by designing schools that are progressive, rigorous and joyful for children. We aim to create visionary leaders who drive a learning environment that ensures success through academic achievement, social & emotional support, and a strong relationship with the community.

Four distinctive leadership
competencies of our program

Our approach is grounded in immersing aspiring School Leaders in all aspects of school leadership through experiential learning and consistent focus on the leadership competencies. These competencies form the foundation of our program and aim to equip our aspiring school leaders with the skills and attitudes necessary to inspire and motivate a team of excellent educators, working together to provide children with a love for learning and life.

Jesal Doshi

Assistant School leader, Natwar Nagar school

“The School Leadership Academy gave me a platform to learn from and interact with excellent educators from different schools in Mumbai and Pune. One of my biggest takeaways was the observation debrief cycle and the session on difficult conversations. Both these help me a lot in my current role of supporting teachers from grades 3 to 5. I have a lot of clarity when I actually plan before having a debrief with the teacher and feel confident to initiate a conversation about difficult situations when needed.”
